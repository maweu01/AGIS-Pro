Overview

One file.
Everything you need.

AeroGIS Pro v4 is a zero-dependency, single-file HTML application that delivers a complete aerial survey planning and environmental intelligence platform. No server, no build process, no npm. Open index.html in any modern browser and the entire system — maps, aircraft database, KCAA registry, and the 7-step Environmental Planning Lab — is immediately available.

ℹ
Architecture The complete application ships as a single index.html file (181 KB). It uses Leaflet 1.9.4 and Leaflet Draw 1.0.4 from CDN, the Open-Meteo public weather API (no key required), and Esri World Services for basemaps and hillshade. All logic is ES5 JavaScript — no transpiler, no bundler, no framework.

Core Modules

Nine modules.
Unified workspace.

Draw a survey polygon once — it flows automatically into the flight planner, site assessment engine, change detection module, Environmental Planning Lab, and every export format. All nine modules share the same Leaflet map canvas and data state.

✈️
Flight Planner
Five preset survey shapes — rectangle, L-shape, triangle, corridor, circle — plus freehand polygon draw. Adjustable altitude (0–500 m), camera FOV (30–120°), side overlap (50–95%), forward overlap (50–95%), and wind direction (0–315°). Generates parallel lawnmower flight lines with correct swath spacing. All shapes are fully resizable by dragging Leaflet Draw vertex handles after placement.
MISSION PLANNING
📐
Coordinate Entry
Five precision input methods: manual point-by-point lat/lng entry, bounding box (N/S/E/W), circle by centre coordinate and radius (minimum 10 m), WKT polygon import using standard POLYGON((lng lat,...)) syntax, and DMS converter supporting the format 1°17'10.8"S 36°49'2"E. All methods produce resizable editable polygons immediately ready for flight planning.
PRECISION INPUT
🏗️
Site Assessment
Six automated assessment types: Real Estate (development potential, plot yield, infrastructure cost); Urban Planning (population capacity, road length, water demand); Solar Feasibility (kWp capacity, annual generation kWh, KSH revenue); Flood Risk (return periods, drainage coefficient, mitigation measures); Soil & Agriculture (Nitisols classification, pH, crop suitability); Infrastructure (tarmac km, grid proximity, 4G coverage). Each produces a scored suitability report with KPIs derived from the drawn survey area.
ANALYSIS
🚧
Airspace Intelligence
Pre-loaded Kenya NFZ/airspace database: JKIA Airport CTR (8 km), Wilson Airport CTZ (3 km), Embakasi Military Zone (2 km), State House Government Restricted Area (1.5 km), Nairobi National Park buffer (500 m). Danger zones rendered in red, warning zones in amber. All zones visualised on the map with radius circles and centroid markers that open info popups with type, radius, and enforcement level.
SAFETY
🔄
Change Detection
Six bi-temporal analysis types against a Jan 2024 → Mar 2026 baseline: Land Use Change (94% confidence, vegetation loss vs. built gain); Deforestation Detection (canopy loss hectares, CO₂ quantification); Illegal Mining (site count, NEMA alert trigger); Flood Mapping (SAR Sentinel-1 inundation extent, depth range); Urban Expansion (growth rate %/yr); Bathymetric LiDAR (532 nm green + 1064 nm IR dual-wavelength, 40 m water depth, 5–20 pts/m²). Source: Sentinel-2 10 m.
MONITORING
📡
Drone Log & Telemetry
Live telemetry dashboard with six real-time channels: altitude AGL (m), ground speed (m/s), battery % (with low-battery colour warning), compass heading (°), RC signal strength (%), and total distance flown (m). Animated canvas chart renders altitude profile and battery decay curve simultaneously. Demo flight mode animates a marker along the generated waypoints at 500 ms intervals. Flight history stores four pre-loaded Kenya missions; supports CSV/JSON import.
TELEMETRY
🔌
Aircraft Connection
Three hardware connection protocols: Web Bluetooth BLE — pairs with DJI RC controllers, Herelink, Skydroid, FrSky (requires Chrome/Chromium); WebSocket MAVLink — connects to Mission Planner, QGroundControl, or ArduPilot running on the local WiFi network at a user-specified host:port; DJI Cloud API — SDK integration for Phantom 4 RTK, Mavic 3 Enterprise, Matrice 300 RTK, and Matrice 400 via DJI developer account.
CONNECTIVITY
🪪
KCAA Pilot Registry
Full licence management system with five pre-loaded Kenya RPAS operators. Search by pilot name or licence ID; filter by registered drone model and operation type (VLOS / BVLOS / Night / Urban / Survey). Multi-criteria mission validation cross-checks licence class against planned altitude, checks medical and licence expiry dates with 30-day advance warning, and verifies survey operation authorisation. New pilot registration form. Compliant with KCAA LN 182/2022.
COMPLIANCE
🌍
Environmental Planning Lab
Full-stack 7-step GIS modelling pipeline: Satellite Ingestion → DEM Extraction → Watershed Delineation → SWAT/HEC-RAS Hydrology → MCE Suitability → Urban Zoning → Cartographic Render. Supports four preset Kenya study regions plus unlimited freehand custom AOI drawing anywhere on the map. Generates a professional cartographic master plan with north arrow, scale bar, legend, graticule, and OSM base map, downloadable as PNG/PDF. EMCA Cap. 387 compliant.

Environmental Planning Lab

Satellite to master plan
in seven steps.

The EPL implements a professional-grade GIS modelling pipeline that takes raw satellite imagery and DEM data as inputs and produces a county-ready land-use zoning plan, complete with hydrological analysis, multi-criteria land suitability evaluation, and a print-quality cartographic master plan document.

01
Satellite Ingestion
Band compositing, cloud masking, and NDVI calculation on Sentinel-2 10 m imagery. Supports Sentinel-2 (free), Landsat 8, Planet Labs 3 m, and WorldView-3 0.3 m. Band combination selection: B2, B3, B4, B8 (RGB + NIR).
02
DEM Extraction & Terrain Analysis
SRTM 30 m, ALOS 12.5 m, or Copernicus 30 m elevation model ingestion. Slope, aspect, hillshade, and curvature derivative computation. Esri World Hillshade tile overlay activated at 62% opacity over satellite base, with terrain-irregularity contour rings, spot elevation markers, and slope aspect arrows.
S = √ ( (∂z/∂x)² + (∂z/∂y)² )
03
Watershed & Drainage Delineation
D8 flow direction algorithm (Jenson & Domingue, 1988) applied to a depression-filled DEM. Flow accumulation raster, stream network extraction, pour-point watershed delineation. Drainage density calculated in km/km². Tools: GRASS GIS r.watershed, GDAL, WhiteboxTools.
04
Hydrological Modelling
SWAT+ simulation outputs: annual runoff 485 mm/yr, baseflow 120 mm/yr, evapotranspiration 840 mm/yr, peak flow 380 m³/s. HEC-RAS 1D hydraulic analysis: water surface elevation 12.4 m, flow velocity 1.8 m/s, Manning's n 0.045. 100-year flood extent delineated and added to the composite map.
05
Multi-Criteria Suitability Evaluation (MCE)
User-adjustable weighted overlay across four criteria: elevation, flood risk, slope, and distance to water. Weights tunable via live sliders (must sum to 100%). Raster suitability grid classified to five levels: Very High → Very Low. Can be recalculated independently from the full pipeline after weight adjustments.
Suitability = Σ ( wᵢ × cᵢ ) for i = 1 … 4
06
Urban Zoning Generation
Raster suitability classification converted to polygon vectors. Eight land-use zone types assigned from MCE scores. Zone area statistics computed in hectares. Individual zone visibility toggle and opacity control. GeoJSON export with full attribute table: zone_id, zone_label, area_ha, description, CRS EPSG:4326.
07
Cartographic Rendering & Master Plan
A3 landscape canvas (1754 × 1240 px) with white page, cream map background, OSM-style road network (primary + secondary roads with casing and fill), buildings, and vegetation beneath semi-transparent zone polygons. Conservation and wetland zones use diagonal hatch fill per British OS convention. North arrow, alternating-block scale bar (0–4 km, 1:50,000), coordinate graticule labels, and a seven-section legend — all inside the double-rule neatline frame. Times New Roman font throughout. Exports as PNG download plus print-to-PDF preview window.

✏️
Drawing a Custom AOI — Step by Step 1. Open the Planning Lab and select Custom AOI from Map in the region dropdown. 2. Click Draw AOI in the EPL map toolbar — the cursor changes to a crosshair. 3. Click on the map to place polygon vertices. A green preview line closes to the first point. 4. Double-click to finish the polygon. The boundary is locked and area calculated from real geodetic bounds. 5. The seven-step pipeline then runs entirely within the bounds of your drawn area. The model parameters, MCE suitability grid, zone polygons, and master plan all adapt automatically to the actual centre and extent of your custom AOI.

First Mission — 5 Steps
Step 1 — Select Survey Area
Click a preset shape (Rect / L / Triangle / Corridor / Circle) in the SURVEY sidebar, or click Custom Draw to polygon any area on the map.
Step 2 — Choose Platform & Sensor
Switch to the AIRCRAFT sidebar tab. Select drone category, model, camera type, and sensor. Specs auto-populate and altitude limit enforces KCAA class.
Step 3 — Set Flight Parameters
Adjust altitude, FOV, side overlap, front overlap, and wind direction sliders. The swath width and line spacing update live.
Step 4 — Generate Flight Plan
Click Generate Flight Plan. Parallel flight lines with waypoints appear on the map. Mission Summary panel shows lines, waypoints, area, swath, GSD, and estimated image count.
Step 5 — Export
Use the Quick Export buttons in the Mission Summary panel, or switch to the Export tab for the full format menu including Mission Planner, DJI Pilot 2, and Litchi.
